the adoption of quality by design (QbD) and analytical method lifestyle management (AMLM) concepts to ensure the quality of pharmaceutical products has been applied and proposed the last few years.These concepts are based on knowledge gained from the application of scientific and quality risk manageement approches throughout method lifecycle to assure continous improvement and high reliabilityof analytical results.The oveeall AMLM starts with the definition of the method, intentend use through the analytical target profile definition,including three stages:(1) method Design, taking advcantages of the well-known concept of QbD;(2)Method intended use through use analytivcal target profile definition, including three stages: (1) Method Design , taking advantages of the well known concept of QbD:(2) Method Performance Qualification: (3) continued method performance verification. This is intended to holistically align method variability with product requirements , increasing confidence in the data generated, a regulatoruy requirement that the pharmaceutical industry must follow. This approach views all method-related activities , such as development , validation , transfer, and routine use as a continum and interrelated process, where knowledge and risk management are the key enablers. An increase in method robustness, cost reduction, and decreased risk failures are some of the intrinsic benefits from this lifestyle management. This approach is clearly acknowledged both by thge regulators and industry. The roadmap of the regulatory and industry events that mark the evolution of these concepts helps to capture the current and future expectation of the pharmaceurtical framework.
